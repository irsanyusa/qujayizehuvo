Snoop Dogg Announces 'High School Reunion Tour' With Wiz Khalifa

TALENTRECAP.COM Entertainment Celebrity

Photo by Harmony Gerber/WireImage

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Snoop Dogg recently revealed that he will be embarking on a summer tour across North America. The High School Reunion tour will visit 33 cities, and will feature a star-studded lineup of artists. Artists include Wiz Khalifa, Too $hort, Warren G, Berner, and special guest DJ Drama.

Snoop Dogg Announces Summer Tour

With a lineup of some of the biggest names in hip-hop, this tour isn’t one to miss. The High School Reunion tour is an opportunity to see two of the biggest names in hip-hop perform together once again.

Joining Snoop Dogg and Wiz Khalifa on the tour are Too $hort, Warren G, Berner, and special guest DJ Drama. The tour guarantees fans an unforgettable experience as all the artists bring their unique style and energy to the stage.

One of the highlights of the tour will be at the Barclays Center on August 4. This show will feature some of the biggest names in hip-hop in one of New York City’s most iconic venues.

Tickets for all dates go on sale Friday, March 10, and also a city presale starting today, Tuesday, March 7. To ensure they don’t miss out on the highly anticipated tour, fans should secure their tickets early as they can.

The Tour Will Cover a Total of 31 Cities

Snoop Dogg, Wiz Khalifa, Too $hort, Warren G, Berner, and DJ Drama are all set to embark on a North American Summer tour later this year. The tour kicks off on July 7 at Rogers Arena in Vancouver and will wrap up on August 27 at FivePoint Amphitheatre in Irvine, California.

The tour will cover a total of 31 cities, with stops in Ridgefield, Washington; Denver, Colorado; Brooklyn, New York, and more. Fans can also expect an electrifying performance from the artists, and their high-energy shows and crowd-pleasing hits.

Snoop Dogg will undoubtedly perform some of his classic hits like “Gin and Juice,” and “Nuthin’ but a G Thang.” Wiz Khalifa is sure to perform fan favorites like “Black and Yellow,” and “See You Again.”
