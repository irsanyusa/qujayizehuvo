Juventus legends lambast 1 player following stunning Champions League exit at expense of FC Porto

- Cristiano Ronaldo is under heavy criticism for being responsible for Juve’s Champions League exit

- Juventus were dumped out of the tournament by highly impressive FC Porto

- Club legends Fabio Capello and Del Piero are not happy with Ronaldo’s performance on the night

Following Juventus exit in the second round of this season’s Champions League, club legends Fabio Capello and Alessandro Del Piero have taken a swipe on Cristiano Ronaldo.

The striker has been criticised for being responsible for Porto’s second goal as the Portuguese club dump the Old Lady out of the competition.

Juventus were kicked out of the competition despite the Old Lady's 3-2 win in the second leg.

The reverse fixture had ended 2-1 in favour of the Portuguese club, which means Porto go through to the next stage courtesy the away goals' rule after it ended 4-4 on aggregates.

Ronaldo and his Juve teammates failed to find a way past 38-year-old Pepe and his dogged defensive colleagues, who held on to claim a famous victory in Turin.

Sergio Oliveira’s free-kick from about 30-yards beat Wojciech Szczesny to end the Old Lady’s dreams, but Ronaldo was singled-out for allowing the goal.

He was part of the four-man wall that the goalkeeper prepared or the defence of the freekick, but the ‘wall’ failed after players, including CR7, turned their back as the ball went straight into the net.

And their ex-manager Capello blasted the Portuguese superstar for not being braver. He said via Mirror:

"Certain goals cannot be conceded. Cristiano Ronaldo jumping and turning in the wall... whoever is in the wall cannot be afraid of being hit."It is an unforgivable mistake that has no excuse."

Former Juventus forward Del Piero was also less than impressed, and also pointed out a golden chance from a header Ronaldo missed earlier in the game. He said:
