Jenna Johnson, Val Chmerkovskiy Film Cute Video with Their Baby Rome

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent Dancing With The Stars

Photo by Michael Tullberg/Getty Images

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Jenna Johnson and Val Chmerkovskiy alongside their son Rome are surely enjoying their time as a little family. Johnson recently made a cute video with her boys as they happily enjoy music together.

Jenna Johnson Shares Adorable Family Video

In a recent Instagram post, Johnson shared how she and her husband have been dealing with their early bird son, Rome. The Dancing With The Stars couple gave a glimpse of their situation through a clips of them moving to music with their baby.

“Daylight savings with a baby be like. Any new parents agree?!” she wrote.

Upon seeing the video, fans expressed their happy reactions to the cute family. They are particularly charmed by Rome who indeed looks game to join his parents in the video.

“Rome is like I’m just here for the vibes lol,” one fan wrote.

“OMG! Love this and Rome is so cute!” another fan commented.

On January 10, Johnson and Chmerkovskiy welcomed Rome to the world. The couple announced the good news on Instagram with a black-and-white photo of their newborn’s hand next to their own. Since then, the two have been sharing updates about little Rome on their social media accounts.

Rome is a Vampire for His First Ever Halloween

On Halloween, Johnson took the time to dress up Rome for the holiday for the first time. She shared a series of his snaps to her Instagram, holding up her baby as he was donning his vampire costume.

In the photos, Rome wore a velvet cape, white collared shirt and black pants. Meanwhile, Johnson slipped on a skeleton shirt and blue wig.

“Our little Vampire’s first Halloween,” she wrote.

In another post, Johnson also showed how the DWTS judging panel attempted to fake score Rome’s costume. Their baby got a 33 out of 40.

During Instagram Q&A over the summer, Johnson admitted how their way of raising Rome was affected by their “different belief systems.” According to the DWTS pro, it became “very hard” to navigate parenting at the beginning.

“I think we had to face the fact that we needed to have open and honest communication constantly so neither of us was offended or hurt by the other if we didn’t see eye to eye on something. But!!! Now it is one of my favorite aspects about our relationship, that we are able to experience such a cultured life together and learn about one another’s upbringing,” she wrote.
