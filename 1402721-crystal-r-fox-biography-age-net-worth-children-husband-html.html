Career

Her professional career started in the late 1970s when she played in some stage productions in Atlanta, Georgia. However, she debuted on the big screen in 1989, when she appeared in a comedy-drama movie "Driving Miss Daisy" alongside Morgan Freeman.

The success came to her when she was cast as Luann Corbin on the NBC police drama show "In the Heat of the Night" and became a regular cast member, appearing in over 100 episodes of the series.

She continued being a productive actress in the 1990s, as she had a few supportive and guest-star roles, alongside the main ones. Additionally, she has appeared in multiple stage productions, mostly in Atlanta, which is her place of residence.
